Discussion Questions

Use Mathbreakers as a conversation-starter for tough topics like negatives and fractions.


The following questions go along with each level so you can be in step with your students as they play and learn.

Level 1 (B1 Intro) This level will get your students familiar with moving around and interacting with numbers.

Teacher's note: When two numbers touch they add. If the result is zero, the numbers disappear. Positive numbers are white, while negatives are black.



- What happens when two numbers touch?

- What if a bigger number touches a smaller number?

- Can you tell me what makes numbers disappear?		 Addition
Level 2 (B2 Adding) Now that your students are good at the controls, the adding problems get a little harder.

- Can you tell the difference bewteen even and odd numbers?

- Why is it important? (Can you break a 3 wall with a combination of 2s?)

- Can you make two numbers disappear if they're the same color?		 Addition
Level 3 (B3 Countryside) Here we introduce numbers up to 52.

- How can you use two big numbers to get a single small number like "1"?

- What did the red doorway do? (It multiplies or divides)		 Addition
Level 4 (B4 Wildlife) This level requires a bit of higher thinking and two step addition processes.

- How can you break a wall that has two different numbers?

- Did anyone break the 1024 wall? How?		 Addition
Level 5 (C1 Operators) Multiplication is introduced in this lesson.

- What happens when you go backwards through a number hoop? (The red hoop)

- How can you make a 5 out of a 1, using just a x2 and x3 hoop?		 Multiplication
Level 6 (C2 Castle) A bit more complicated operator logic.

- How can you make a 3 with starting with a 4 and a x2, x3 and x5 hoop?

- What strategy did you use to break the two 9 walls?		 Multiplication
Level 7 (c3 Giant Spikey) More complicated operator logic, culminating in a 128 spikey boss.

- How did you defeat the 128 boss?

- What do you notice about the number 2?		 Multiplication
Level 8 (D1 Fractions) Here we introduce two new machines, the elevator and the bridge, which require the use of fractions to operate.

- How can you make a staircase?

- How does the bridge work? (You must add to 1 with blue triangle numbers)		 Fractions
Level 9 (D2 Sword) Here we give students the fraction sword which cuts numbers into equal halves.

- How can you make a 1/2 when only given a bunch of 1/3 and a sword? (You add 1/3 + 1/3 + 1/3 and chop it in half to get 1/2 + 1/2)

- What happens when you keep chopping a number in half?

- After chopping a 1 in half a bunch of times, can you return it back to a 1?		 Fractions
Level 10 (D3 Risers) This level focuses on the number line with elevators.

- When you make a staircase, what is important to remember about each step?

- Did anyone find the jetpack?		 Fractions
Level 11 (D4 Rockets) This level reinforces fractions with both hoops and the sword.

- How can you get to 1/10?

- Did anyone find the spellbook?		 Fractions
Level 12 (D5 Boss) This is the end of the game for now, requiring multiple puzzles of adding fractions to "1".

- Name three different fractions that you can add together to get to 1.		 Addition
